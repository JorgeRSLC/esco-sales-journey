Proposal submission.

ESCO submits the proposal and follows up on a fixed cadence. Three outcome paths — two fully defined, two placeholders for future deep dives.

Follow-up cadence

After submission, ESCO follows up at 5 business days, then again at 10 business days (another 5 after the first). The cadence is fixed — same for every prospect, regardless of state.

Path A — Proposal accepted

Typical sequence:

  • Prospect requests references.
  • Prospect negotiates terms.
  • Contract signed.

Timeline to close varies significantly based on the internal mechanisms of the account. No fixed ESCO-side interval applies here.

Path B — Proposal rejected

Rejection can come in two forms:

  • Direct rejection — the prospect declines without engaging further.
  • Post-negotiation rejection — the prospect goes through references or negotiation, then declines.

Placeholder   Post-rejection pathway to be developed in a separate deep dive.

Path C — No clear decision

No clear message of acceptance or rejection received. The prospect goes quiet, or responds ambiguously.

Placeholder   Structure to be developed in a separate deep dive.

Open questions

  • For Path C, what's the decision rule for when "no response" tips into "lost"?
  • Do we capture the reason for rejection (Path B) in a structured way, and if so, how does it feed back into future outreach?
  • Does a Path B prospect re-enter nurture, or go to a different bucket?

State at Stage 04

Entered Stage 04 with state refined at Stage 03. State influences expectations but not process — the follow-up cadence is fixed.
