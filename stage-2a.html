Nurture offer.

After a declined evaluation, ESCO asks to stay in touch on a regular cadence. Initial offer: every 60 days.

How it works

If the prospect says no to the evaluation, ESCO asks for permission to contact them at a regular cadence. The default offer is every 60 days, and that cadence holds constant unless the prospect explicitly requests to accelerate or decelerate.

The four response types

Each response type carries its own state signal:

  • Eager — looks forward to hearing from ESCO. Likely semi-critical.
  • Specific — sets a specific date or alternative cadence. Likely semi-critical.
  • Hedging — accepts without much interest, or asks to extend the cadence. Likely non-critical.
  • Declines — opts out of nurture altogether. Non-critical. Drops to a 3-year check-in cadence unless they reach out first.

Where it goes next

  • Eager / Specific / Hedging → Stage 02b · Nurture path (active 60-day cadence, or adjusted per request).
  • Declines → 3-year check-in cadence.

Open questions

  • For "specific" responders who set an alternative cadence, do we track the cadence they proposed, or normalize everything to 60 days in reporting?
  • What's the right script for re-confirming interest at each nurture touch?
