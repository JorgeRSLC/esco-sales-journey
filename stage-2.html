Response to offer.

The prospect's answer to the initial ask — and the urgency behind it — provides our first read on state.

The two responses

Yes — accepts evaluation

The prospect is at least semi-critical, possibly critical. Urgency is itself a second-order signal: sooner = more critical.

  • ESCO targets a walkthrough within one week, but the interval is typically set by the prospect.
  • Prospects pushing for very short windows (days, not weeks) read as critical.
  • Proceeds to Stage 03 · Walkthrough.

No — declines evaluation

The prospect is most likely non-critical. This is not a dead end — it opens the door to a nurture relationship.

  • Proceeds to Stage 02a · Nurture offer.
  • The "no" is a signal, not a final state. Facility conditions, leadership changes, and vendor dissatisfaction can all flip it.

Open questions

  • How do we document the urgency signal? A structured field or a free-text note?
  • Is there a third response we see in practice — "not now, but send info" — that deserves its own branch?

State Signals

  • Yes + fast schedule Critical
  • Yes + normal schedule Semi-critical
  • No Non-critical
