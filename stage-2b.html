Nurture path.

Once on the nurture cadence, the process runs indefinitely unless the prospect explicitly exits. We're watching for state-change signals.

The default state

A prospect in Stage 02b is on a 60-day cadence (or whatever cadence they set) that continues indefinitely. No action required unless a signal arrives.

State-change signals

State is detected through explicit or implicit signals during nurture contact:

  • Requests for shorter follow-up intervals.
  • Acceptance of a walkthrough when offered.
  • Communication of dissatisfaction with their current vendor or setup.

The two transitions

  • State shifts to semi-critical or critical, prospect accepts a proposal offer → enters Stage 03 · Walkthrough. This is the gated transition up to the proposal lane.
  • Declines all future outreach → exits active nurture, moves to 3-year check-in cadence.

Open questions

  • How do we capture soft signals (tone, unreturned calls, turnover at the account) that don't rise to explicit state change but suggest we should re-probe?
  • Is there a practical upper bound on how long a prospect can stay in nurture without any signal before we re-offer the evaluation?

State Signals

  • Shorter interval requested Semi-critical
  • Accepts walkthrough Critical
  • Vendor dissatisfaction Critical
  • No signal Non-critical

Timing

Default cadence 60 days
Duration Indefinite
Exit cadence 3 years

Outputs

  • State shift · accepts offer → Stage 03 · Walkthrough (gated)
  • Declines outreach → 3-year check-in terminus
  • No signal → Remains on 60-day cadence
